# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Shoppers in Egypt (EGP, Arabic-friendly, WhatsApp-first support) browsing everyday
fine accessories — stainless-steel jewelry that doesn't tarnish. They arrive on the
homepage to discover, not to transact immediately: they want to feel the brand is
trustworthy and tasteful before they buy. Mobile-heavy traffic.

## Product Purpose

The storefront homepage is the brand's first impression and primary merchandising
surface. It must make affordable, everyday jewelry feel considered and desirable —
surfacing best sellers, on-sale pieces, a time-bound flash sale, and category entry
points — while reading as a boutique, not a bargain bin. Success = visitors trust the
brand at a glance and move into the catalog.

## Brand Personality

Quiet luxury. Restrained, precise, warm. Confident without shouting. Voice is calm
and tasteful — "considered," not "hyped." Emotional goal: the unhurried confidence of
a small boutique where everything is chosen with care. Generous negative space; slow,
subtle motion; warmth carried by material (cream, brown, gold), imagery, and
typography rather than by loud color or urgency.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI storefront** — cream bg + reflex serif (Fraunces/Playfair) + a tiny
  uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section. The 2026 AI-default look. Banned.
- **Busy marketplace** (Amazon/Shein) — dense grids, stacked banners, zero breathing room.
- **Cold minimal tech / SaaS-clone** — sterile, personality-free, gray-on-white.
- **Loud discount energy** — aggressive red "SALE!", countdown-as-pressure, cheapening urgency.

## Design Principles

1. **Restraint is the luxury.** Negative space, few elements per fold, one idea at a time.
   When unsure, remove.
2. **Warmth without noise.** Carry warmth through material (cream/brown/gold), real
   product imagery, and characterful type — never through loud color or urgency.
3. **Material honesty.** Let product photography be the hero; chrome and ornament step back.
4. **Distinctive, not decorated.** Differentiation comes from typographic voice and
   precise spacing, not from stickers, eyebrows, gradients, or effects.
5. **Calm motion.** Slow ease-out, subtle reveals that fit what they reveal; nothing
   that reads as pressure or flash. Honor reduced-motion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 (no light-gray-on-cream), large text ≥3:1. Visible
focus-visible rings on all interactive elements. Full keyboard operability. Honor
`prefers-reduced-motion`. Alt text on imagery; aria-labels on icon-only controls.
RTL-aware where Arabic content appears.
